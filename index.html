Hi, I'm Michael. (Not Mike.)

I live in Boston and work at Gearflow. I studied CS at Cornell and was many times a fellow at IDEO CoLab. I like competitive things and I race for Full Send Triathlon.

How might we conduct research outside the walls of academia? I reject the idea that you need the validation of an institution to do valuable research. Currently Im interested in research around making playful creative tools for learning experiences. People spend large amounts of time and effort playing games. Can these games teach valuable skills? Runescape teaches it's players economics. Minecraft teaches logic through redstone. Can we intentionally create games that educate while being _actually fun_?

I'm interested in a lot of things. I'm not yet sure if this is a good thing or not. It's one of my questions. Either way, I maintain a backlog of things I am interested in. Contact me if you'd like to talk about any of them; I absolutely love making friends online.
